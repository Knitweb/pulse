Field data · Solid pod · WebID · Vank

Your originals stay in your vault. The fabric carries only proof.

An AR glass on the Knitweb sees the world and shares what it verified — never what it filmed. Every capture, location trace and scan lives in your Solid pod, behind your access control. What crosses the p2p web is a signed, content-addressed digest: enough for any peer to verify a sighting, never enough to reconstruct your camera roll.

One sighting, two homes

The raw frame goes to the wearer's pod at a path that is its SHA-256 digest. The fabric gets a float-free record carrying that digest, a geohash cell, and the observer's signature — verify-before-trust, end to end.

How a sighting travels

  1. Recognize. The glass resolves a physical thing to a Web node CID — a QR/ArUco marker (exact), an object-detection label, or a nearest-neighbour embedding. Probabilistic matches carry their confidence and cannot bind durably without an explicit confirmation step.
  2. Vault the original. The raw capture is stored in the wearer's Solid pod at field/captures/<sha256>. The path is the digest, so the pod reference and the record corroborate each other by construction.
  3. Weave the proof. A float-free field-observation record — geohash cell, altitude band, label, integer confidence, target CID, observer address, optional pod_ref + capture_digest — is signed and woven, together with a spatial anchor for "what's near me?" queries.
  4. Share peer-to-peer. Attested observations travel as one canonical bundle over any carrier — BitChat/BLE between buren, WebRTC across the wider web. Verification is all-or-nothing: one forged entry refuses the whole bundle.
  5. Ask the vault, not the wire. A peer who needs the original resolves observer → WebID → pod and asks. The owner grants or refuses through their pod's own access control. Revoking access is a pod setting — the fabric never held the pixels.

The same separation the Worlds Narrow Web applies to identity — raw PII is never stored, only content-addressed digests — applied to what a glass sees.

WebID: one wearer, two names, one signature

A wearer signs observations with a pls1 key and owns a pod that answers to a WebID. The webid-link record ties them together — signed by the key itself, so nobody can claim someone else's vault or hang their vault on someone else's key. Re-linking is just a newer beat. 

// woven, attested, latest beat wins
{
  "kind":     "webid-link",
  "webid":    "https://alice.pod.example/profile/card#me",
  "observer": "pls1q8f0…",   // address of the signing key
  "beat":     7             // integer Pulse time
}

Verify before trust

A link is a plain read; acting on it requires checking its attestation — the same validate-at-read gate every fabric item passes.

Pairwise-friendly

Personhood stays digest-only. The link carries an address and a URL — no name, no e-mail, no phone, nothing to leak.

Portable

Move pods? Weave a new link at a later beat. Peers resolving observer → WebID follow you automatically.

Vank: ask, grant, settle — without custody

Vank is the exchange end of the WNW spectrum: markets without a central wallet or hosted orderbook as trust root. Applied to field data, it is how access and effort meet: a peer posts a signed intent for what they need — an original capture behind your pod's access control, relay of observation bundles through a neighbourhood, curation of a cell's sightings — and settlement is recorded on the pulse ledger in PLS, the activity unit that meters useful work.

Keys stay client-side

Your narrow-web identity signs intents in your own agent. No operator holds funds, captures, or the right to speak as you.

Pod grants, not copies

What changes hands is access — an ACL grant on your pod — plus a digest that proves what was granted. Revocation stays yours.

Integer settlement

Floats never reach the canonical value path. Work is metered and settled in integer PLS, like every other useful-work flow on the web.

Under the hood

Everything on this page is running code in knitweb/pulse, property-tested: fabric/observation.py (the float-free record), edge/observer.py (the confirmation gate), edge/exchange.py (all-or-nothing p2p bundles), solid/pod.py (the injectable pod-vault seam) and solid/webid.py (the WebID link). The pod bridge is a seam, not an HTTP client — a real Solid backend plugs in exactly like the BLE radio and the WebRTC shell do.
